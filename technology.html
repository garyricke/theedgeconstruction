Accuracy

Precision Laser Layout

Every project — regardless of size — begins with professional-grade rotary laser equipment. This isn't an upsell; it's the only way we work. Laser layout ensures that every track, stud, and ceiling grid goes exactly where the drawings say it should, accurate to fractions of an inch.

Downstream trades — tile, casework, glass, MEP — all depend on framing being right. When framing is off, everything downstream pays for it. We eliminate that problem on day one.

  • Rotary laser equipment standard on every project
  • Complex geometry — curves, angles, multi-story alignment
  • Ceiling grid layouts verified before hanging
  • Documented layout as part of project handoff
Efficiency

In-House Prefabrication Shop

Our dedicated prefabrication shop is one of the clearest competitive advantages we offer. Assemblies — wall panels, soffit frames, header packages, and more — are pre-built off-site in a controlled shop environment and delivered ready to install.

This reduces on-site labor time by up to 75% for prefab-eligible scope, keeps the job site cleaner, and removes variability from the installation process. When quality control happens in the shop — not in the field — it shows in the finished product.

  • Wall panel and soffit assembly pre-built off-site
  • Up to 75% reduction in on-site labor time
  • Shop QC before anything reaches the building
  • Cleaner job sites and faster install sequences

Common Questions

Technology FAQ

BIM (Building Information Modeling) coordination means working from a shared 3D digital model that integrates structural, architectural, MEP, and specialty trade information before a single stud is set. It allows our team to identify clashes and coordination issues in the model — not on the job site. The result is fewer surprises, faster execution, and reduced cost for everyone on the project.

Professional-grade rotary laser equipment establishes layout control points accurate to fractions of an inch across an entire floor plate — far more precise than snap lines or manual measurement. This level of accuracy is critical for complex ceiling systems, curtain wall backup framing, and multi-floor alignment. It eliminates the rework that comes from framing that is even slightly out of position.

Prefabrication means building assemblies — framed walls, drywall panels, header packages — in our controlled shop environment before they go to the job site. Because shop work is faster, cleaner, and more consistent than field work, we can install those assemblies up to 75% faster on site. It also reduces job site waste, noise, and crew congestion, which GCs and owners appreciate on tight-schedule projects.

Our project management platform provides real-time schedule tracking, RFI and submittal management, daily field reports, and issue documentation. GCs have visibility into our progress without having to chase us for updates. Owners get confidence that their schedule commitments are being met. We use the same platform our GC partners prefer — no learning curve, no friction.

Laser layout and Procore run on every project — no exceptions. Prefabrication is used whenever scope, schedule, and logistics allow it to deliver meaningful time savings. Continuous Flow Drywall and the No Coat machine are deployed on applicable finishing scopes. The goal is always the same: fewer surprises, better quality, faster delivery.
